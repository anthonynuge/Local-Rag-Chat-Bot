# Permits and Fees

## Entrance fees

Entrance fees are charged per vehicle or per person at both gates and at the
self-serve kiosks outside staffed hours. Passes cover the calendar day of
purchase, not 24 hours.

| Pass type                           | Price | Notes                              |
|-------------------------------------|-------|------------------------------------|
| Vehicle day pass                    | $22   | Covers everyone in the vehicle     |
| Per-person day pass (shuttle/bike/foot) | $9 | Under 12 free                    |
| Thornmere annual pass               | $65   | Named holder plus one vehicle      |
| Commercial van (up to 15 seats)     | $90   | Requires commercial registration   |
| Motorcycle day pass                 | $14   | Per machine                        |

The annual pass is sold at the Fernline Visitor Center desk and on TrailPass;
gate staff cannot issue it. Fee-free days are announced each January by the
Meridian Parks Authority — historically the park's establishment anniversary
in June and one weekend in late September.

## Camping fees

Frontcountry camping is charged per site per night, with an eight-person,
two-vehicle limit on standard sites.

| Site type                        | Price per night |
|----------------------------------|-----------------|
| Larkspur standard site           | $28             |
| Larkspur lakeside site           | $36             |
| Coldpine standard site           | $18             |
| Group site (up to 24 people)     | $75             |

Larkspur sites are reservation-only through TrailPass. Coldpine is first-come,
first-served, with self-registration envelopes at the host site; card payment
at the host cabin is available 08:00–20:00. The two group sites are at
Larkspur only and can be booked at most 12 months ahead.

## Backcountry permits

Overnight stays anywhere outside the two campgrounds require a backcountry
permit at **$12 per person per night**, booked on TrailPass. The High Country
zone is under a nightly quota from May 15 through September 30; quota releases
happen in two waves, 60 percent four months ahead and the rest seven days
ahead. The **maximum group size on a single backcountry permit is 8 people**,
and groups may not split a larger party across multiple permits to camp
together — rangers check at the designated sites.

A hard-sided bear canister is required for all food and scented items on
every High Country overnight. Approved models are listed on TrailPass, and
canisters can be rented at the Fernline Visitor Center for $5 per night with
a $60 deposit. Hanging food is not an accepted alternative above treeline —
there are no suitable trees.

Permits must be picked up in person or activated on the TrailPass app by
17:00 on the start date, or the booking releases back to the quota. The
permit hang-tag must be displayed on the dash for overnight parking at the
Sable trailhead.

## Special-use permits

Drone flight is prohibited everywhere in the park without a research permit
countersigned by the superintendent — recreational drone use is not
permitted at any location or season. Commercial filming and photography
require a special-use permit at a $250 application fee with a 21-day review;
the wildlife program can attach conditions or deny locations during sensitive
seasons. Weddings and organized ceremonies at Fernline Meadow require a $150
permit booked at least 30 days out, with a 40-guest ceiling and no amplified
sound. Organized athletic events on park trails are not permitted.

## Reservations, changes, and refunds

All reservations — Larkspur sites, group sites, and backcountry permits —
run through the TrailPass system on a six-month rolling booking window,
except group sites (12 months, above). Cancellations made 72 hours or more
before arrival receive a full refund minus a $6 processing fee per booking.
Inside 72 hours, the first night (or the first permit night) is forfeited.
No-shows forfeit the full booking. When the park closes an area for safety —
fire, storm damage, or a wildlife closure — affected bookings are refunded in
full automatically, including the processing fee, usually within five
business days. Fee disputes go to the TrailPass help desk, not to gate staff,
and gate staff cannot issue refunds in any circumstance.
