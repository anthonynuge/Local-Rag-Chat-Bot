Q: Are dogs allowed in the park?
A: Only in two places: the Mirrorbend Lakeshore Path and inside the two campgrounds, always on a leash no longer than 2 meters. Dogs are not allowed on any other trail, in the backcountry, or on the Ridge Runner shuttle, with an exception for recognized service animals, which are allowed everywhere their handler goes.

Q: Is there cell phone coverage in the park?
A: Effectively none beyond the two gates. There are coverage pockets on high points near the boundary, but do not plan around them. For backcountry trips, carry a satellite messenger; rentals are available in Fern Hollow outside the South Gate, not in the park itself.

Q: How often does the shuttle run, and what does it cost?
A: The Ridge Runner shuttle runs every 20 minutes between the South Gate, Larkspur day-use lot, and the Sable trailhead, from May 15 through October 15, first departure 07:00 and last return 19:40. It is free with any valid park pass. Bikes are carried on a first-come rack that holds four.

Q: Is the park open in winter? Which entrances are open?
A: The park never fully closes, but access changes. The South Gate and Fernline Visitor Center operate year-round. The Coldpine Gate is closed to vehicles from November 1 through April 30; beyond it, the Coldpine road becomes an ungroomed ski and snowshoe route. Winter visitors must self-register at the South Gate kiosk for any travel past the Gorge zone.

Q: Can I swim in Mirrorbend Lake?
A: Yes, at the designated beach near Larkspur Campground only. There are no lifeguards anywhere in the park, the lake stays cold all summer, and inflatable toys are prohibited beyond the buoy line. Swimming in the Graywater River and in the gorge pools is prohibited year-round because of current and cold shock.

Q: Can I fish in the park?
A: Yes, with a Meridian Parks Authority fishing license, sold on TrailPass and at the Fernline desk. The Graywater River is catch-and-release only, single barbless hooks. Mirrorbend Lake has a daily limit of 4 trout. The season runs June 1 through October 31 parkwide.

Q: Can I bring my own firewood for camping?
A: Only if it was bought or cut within 40 km of the park, to keep invasive wood-boring insects out. Firewood is sold at both campground host sites for $8 per bundle, and gathering dead wood inside the park is prohibited.

Q: Where can I charge an electric vehicle?
A: Two Level 2 chargers in the Fernline Visitor Center lot at the South Gate, first-come, four-hour limit. There is no charging at Coldpine or at any trailhead, and the Coldpine road has no services of any kind.

Q: Where do campfires belong, and are they ever banned?
A: Fires are allowed only in the metal rings at campground sites and never in the backcountry, where stoves are the only permitted flame. During fire restrictions, announced on TrailPass and posted at the gates, campground fires are banned too, and the ban includes charcoal.

Q: I lost something in the park — who do I contact?
A: The Fernline Visitor Center runs the single lost-and-found for the whole park. Items are held for 60 days. Call the visitor center desk rather than the dispatch line, which is reserved for emergencies.
