Q: Are dogs allowed in the park?
A: Only in two places: the Mirrorbend Lakeshore Path and inside the two campgrounds, always on a leash no longer than 2 meters. Dogs are not allowed on any other trail, in the backcountry, or on the Ridge Runner shuttle, with an exception for recognized service animals, which are allowed everywhere their handler goes. Bring your own waste bags; none are provided at trailheads. On hot days, remember that summer highs at Fernline and the South Gate reach 24-29°C in July and August, so a parked car is not a safe place to leave a dog while you explore leash-only or off-limits areas.

Q: Is there cell phone coverage in the park?
A: Effectively none beyond the two gates. There are coverage pockets on high points near the boundary, but do not plan around them. For backcountry trips, carry a satellite messenger; rentals are available in Fern Hollow outside the South Gate, not in the park itself. If you need help and have no signal, the fastest option is to walk out to a staffed ranger duty station rather than wait for a bar of service.

Q: How often does the shuttle run, and what does it cost?
A: The Ridge Runner shuttle runs every 20 minutes between the South Gate, Larkspur day-use lot, and the Sable trailhead, from May 15 through October 15, first departure 07:00 and last return 19:40. It is free with any valid park pass. Bikes are carried on a first-come rack that holds four. Plan your last return carefully in shoulder season, since there is no shuttle service at all outside the May 15 - October 15 window.

Q: Is the park open in winter? Which entrances are open?
A: The park never fully closes, but access changes. The South Gate and Fernline Visitor Center operate year-round. The Coldpine Gate is closed to vehicles from November 1 through April 30; beyond it, the Coldpine road becomes an ungroomed ski and snowshoe route. Winter visitors must self-register at the South Gate kiosk for any travel past the Gorge zone. The Ridge Runner shuttle does not run in winter, so all winter travel beyond the South Gate is on foot, ski, or snowshoe.

Q: Can I swim in Mirrorbend Lake?
A: Yes, at the designated beach near Larkspur Campground only. There are no lifeguards anywhere in the park, the lake stays cold all summer, and inflatable toys are prohibited beyond the buoy line. Swimming in the Graywater River and in the gorge pools is prohibited year-round because of current and cold shock.

Q: Can I fish in the park?
A: Yes, with a Meridian Parks Authority fishing license, sold on TrailPass and at the Fernline desk. The Graywater River is catch-and-release only, single barbless hooks. Mirrorbend Lake has a daily limit of 4 trout. The season runs June 1 through October 31 parkwide.

Q: Can I bring my own firewood for camping?
A: Only if it was bought or cut within 40 km of the park, to keep invasive wood-boring insects out. Firewood is sold at both campground host sites for $8 per bundle, and gathering dead wood inside the park is prohibited.

Q: Where can I charge an electric vehicle?
A: Two Level 2 chargers in the Fernline Visitor Center lot at the South Gate, first-come, four-hour limit. There is no charging at Coldpine or at any trailhead, and the Coldpine road has no services of any kind.

Q: Where do campfires belong, and are they ever banned?
A: Fires are allowed only in the metal rings at campground sites and never in the backcountry, where stoves are the only permitted flame. During fire restrictions, announced on TrailPass and posted at the gates, campground fires are banned too, and the ban includes charcoal.

Q: I lost something in the park — who do I contact?
A: The Fernline Visitor Center runs the single lost-and-found for the whole park. Items are held for 60 days. Call the visitor center desk rather than the dispatch line, which is reserved for emergencies.

Q: Is the park accessible for visitors who use a wheelchair?
A: The Gorge Rim Loop near South Gate has a paved section that is wheelchair-accessible out to the second viewpoint platform, a 2.1 km out-and-back from the Gorge trailhead behind Fernline Visitor Center. Beyond that point the loop continues on natural surface. None of the other trailheads (Lakeshore, Sable) currently have wheelchair-accessible tread, and the Sable Ridge Trail and Thornmere Summit Route are steep, exposed backcountry routes not suited to mobility devices.

Q: What should I pack for a day trip into the park?
A: Bring more water than you think you need, since the only reliable potable water taps are at the two campgrounds (available May through October) and there is none on the Sable Ridge Trail past Sable Spring at km 4.8. Carry bear spray if you are heading past the Gorge zone, wear long trousers and use permethrin if you'll be in grassy margins of the Gorge zone during tick season (May-September), and pack layers, since the Sable Ridge Trail is exposed above treeline from about km 6 on. Because there is effectively no cell coverage beyond the gates, tell someone your planned route and expected return time before you set out.

Q: Is Thornmere a good park to visit with young kids?
A: The Gorge Rim Loop is the easiest option, a 6.2 km loop with only 190 m of elevation gain, about 2 hours round trip, with three viewpoint platforms over the falls and a paved wheelchair-accessible section for the first 2.1 km. Children under 12 enter free on the per-person day pass. Keep a close eye on kids on the Mirrorbend Lakeshore Path, since it sees the most moose incidents in the park, and keep children within arm's reach in the Coldpine Forest zone, where mountain lions are occasionally sighted at dawn and dusk. The Fernline Visitor Center also runs a seasonal junior ranger program that's an easy add-on activity for families.

Q: What is the weather like, and does it affect trail access?
A: Afternoon thunderstorms build over the High Country in summer; hikers on the Sable Ridge Trail are advised to be off the exposed crest by 14:00 on days with visible storm buildup. The Thornmere Summit Route is only snow-free from mid-July to late September and closes entirely whenever a winter storm advisory is issued for the High Country zone. Check current conditions on the TrailPass conditions layer before any High Country trip.

Q: What do I do with my garbage? Are there trash cans on the trails?
A: There are no trash cans at any trailhead or on any trail past the gates; carry out everything you carry in, including food scraps and pet waste. Trash and recycling bins are provided only at the South Gate/Fernline Visitor Center area and at both campgrounds. Backcountry campers should keep food waste sealed with the rest of their food inside a hard-sided bear canister rather than burying or burning it.

Q: Where can I get drinking water in the park?
A: Both campgrounds, Larkspur and Coldpine, have potable water taps available May through October. Outside that window, or anywhere in the backcountry, treat or filter water from the Graywater River or Mirrorbend Lake before drinking it. On the Sable Ridge Trail, the last reliable water source is Sable Spring at km 4.8; there is none past that point, including at both backcountry campsites further up the trail.

Q: What happens if I need first aid in the park?
A: For a life-threatening emergency, call the 24-hour emergency dispatch line at +1-555-0197. Basic first-aid supplies are kept at the Fernline duty station (South Gate) and the Coldpine ranger station, and rangers responding to an incident carry a trauma kit. There are no first-aid stations at individual trailheads or on the trails themselves, so carry your own basic kit on any hike.

Q: Where are the restrooms?
A: Restrooms are located at the South Gate/Fernline Visitor Center, the Larkspur day-use lot, and at both campgrounds (Larkspur and Coldpine). Vault toilets are provided at the Gorge and Lakeshore trailheads; the Sable trailhead has none, so use the facilities at the South Gate or Larkspur before heading in. There are no restroom facilities beyond the trailheads once you're on a trail or in the backcountry.

Q: Can I pay with cash, or is there an ATM in the park?
A: There is no ATM anywhere inside the park. Entrance kiosks accept card only outside staffed hours (staffed 07:00-19:00 in summer); during staffed hours gate attendants can take cash or card. The Fernline desk accepts both cash and card. At Coldpine Campground, self-registration is card payment only at the host cabin, 08:00-20:00.

Q: Is there WiFi anywhere in the park?
A: No. There is no public WiFi at the Fernline Visitor Center, at either campground, or anywhere else inside the park, consistent with the lack of cell coverage beyond the gates. If you need connectivity, Fern Hollow, the town outside the South Gate, has indoor lodging and services, but it is not part of the park.

Q: I'm bringing a school or scout group. What do I need to know?
A: Reserve one of the two group campsites at Larkspur, which hold up to 24 people and can be booked up to 12 months ahead through TrailPass. If your group is arriving by commercial van (up to 15 seats), that has its own day-pass category. Note that organized athletic events on park trails are not permitted, so field days, races, or similar organized competitions need to happen outside the park boundary. Ask at the Fernline desk about scheduling a ranger-led interpretive walk to go with your visit; availability depends on which seasonal rangers are on duty that week.

Q: Can I take photos or video in the park?
A: Personal, non-commercial photography and video are welcome anywhere the public can go. Commercial filming or photography, however, requires a special-use permit with a $250 application fee and a 21-day review period. Recreational drone use is prohibited park-wide in any location or season regardless of purpose; only a research permit countersigned by the superintendent allows drone flight. Getting close to wildlife for a photo counts as luring, which is treated the same as feeding wildlife: a citable $175 fine, with repeat or egregious cases prosecuted and subject to a park ban.

Q: Are there picnic areas?
A: Yes. The main picnic area is at the Larkspur day-use lot beside the shuttle stop, with tables near the Lakeshore trailhead. A smaller set of picnic tables is at the South Gate/Fernline Visitor Center area near the Gorge trailhead. There are no picnic tables at the Sable trailhead or in the Coldpine Forest zone.

Q: Are bicycles allowed on the trails?
A: Bikes are welcome on the paved park roads and can be carried on the Ridge Runner shuttle's first-come rack, which holds four. They are not permitted on any of Thornmere's hiking trails, including the Gorge Rim Loop, the Mirrorbend Lakeshore Path, and the Sable Ridge Trail. None of the three trailheads have dedicated bike parking beyond the shuttle stops, so cyclists riding in should lock up near the trailhead signboard and continue on foot.

Q: Can I rent a canoe or kayak at Mirrorbend Lake?
A: Yes. Summit & Pine Outfitters, the park's lakeshore concessionaire, rents canoes from the launch near Larkspur Campground and also runs the adjacent snack stand; ask at their stand for current boat availability, since rental pricing is set by the concessionaire rather than the park. Boats are limited to Mirrorbend Lake itself. The Graywater River and the gorge pools are closed to all watercraft, the same way they're closed to swimming.

Q: I'd like to run a concession or vendor stand in the park. Who do I talk to?
A: New concession opportunities are handled through the superintendent's office at the Fernline administrative annex, not through gate staff or the visitor center desk. The park's one existing concession, Summit & Pine Outfitters, operates the lakeshore canoe rental and snack stand under a five-year agreement; any new commercial arrangement goes through a similar formal review, comparable in spirit to the $250 application fee and 21-day review required for a commercial filming or photography permit.

Q: What's the best time of day to visit for sunrise or sunset?
A: The South Gate is staffed 07:00-19:00 in summer, with card-only kiosk access outside those hours, so an early sunrise start or a late sunset finish is still possible on a valid pass. The Fernline Visitor Center itself keeps shorter hours, 09:00-17:00 daily May through October, so don't count on the desk being open before dawn or after dusk. Cell coverage is effectively nonexistent beyond the gates, so tell someone your plan before heading out in the dark.

Q: When is bug season, and where is it worst?
A: Ticks are active May through September in the grassy margins of the Gorge zone; wear long trousers, use permethrin, and do a full check the same evening. Mosquitoes are worst around the Mirrorbend Lakeshore Path during the June snowmelt flooding, when standing water collects along the shoreline, so pack repellent for a June lakeshore visit.

Q: Does the altitude affect visitors?
A: Thornmere's elevation ranges from about 1,150 m at Fernline and the South Gate up to Mount Thornmere's 2,914 m summit, with local treeline around 2,050 m and the High Country zone starting at 2,100 m. Visitors coming from lower elevations can feel short of breath or tire faster once they're above treeline. Pace yourself on the Sable Ridge Trail's exposed sections above roughly km 6, and drink more water than you would at lower elevation.

Q: Are there quiet hours at the campgrounds?
A: Yes, quiet hours run 22:00 to 06:00 at both Larkspur and Coldpine campgrounds. Generators are permitted only between 08:00 and 20:00 and must be off during quiet hours. Neither campground has electrical hookups, so a generator is the only way to run powered equipment overnight at a site.

Q: Can I run a generator at my campsite?
A: Generators are allowed at both Larkspur and Coldpine campgrounds between 08:00 and 20:00 only, and must be shut off during the 22:00-06:00 quiet hours. Since neither campground has hookups, a generator is the only way to power equipment at a site; keep it at your own site and be considerate of neighboring campers.

Q: What time is check-in and check-out at the campgrounds?
A: Check-in is 14:00 and check-out is 11:00 at both Larkspur and Coldpine. At Coldpine, first-come sites are self-registered using the envelopes at the host cabin, with card payment taken there 08:00-20:00. At Larkspur, reservations are made in advance through TrailPass, so check-in is simply arriving and setting up at your assigned site after 14:00.

Q: Is it safe to leave valuables at my campsite or in my car at a trailhead?
A: There are no lockers or secure storage at any trailhead or campground; the metal food lockers at campsites are for food and scented items only, not valuables. Leave anything you're not willing to lose at home, and don't leave valuables visible inside a parked vehicle at any trailhead, including Sable, where overnight parking for permitted backcountry trips is common.

Q: Where are the best spots to see wildlife?
A: The Mirrorbend Lakeshore Path sees the most moose activity in the park, especially around dawn and dusk. The Coldpine Forest zone is where the rare mountain lion sightings occur, also mostly at dawn and dusk. The Gorge zone hosts nesting peregrine falcons, which is why the trail section between marker posts Gorge 9 and Gorge 11 typically closes for about two weeks each June; check trailhead signboards for current closures before heading out. Whatever you see, keep your distance and never feed or approach wildlife, which is a citable $175 fine per incident.

Q: Are there junior ranger or interpretive programs for kids?
A: The Fernline Visitor Center runs a junior ranger program seasonally, matching the visitor center's May-through-October operating season; ask at the desk for the current activity booklet. Ranger-led interpretive programs run on the same seasonal schedule, staffed by the seasonal ranger pool that works the park May 1 through October 15.

Q: Are there guided walks I can join?
A: Ranger-led guided walks run seasonally, generally May through October, departing from the Fernline Visitor Center at the South Gate. Ask at the visitor center desk for the current week's schedule and meeting point, since walk offerings depend on which seasonal rangers are on duty that week.

Q: Is the Ridge Runner shuttle wheelchair accessible?
A: Yes, shuttle vehicles are wheelchair accessible. The shuttle runs every 20 minutes between the South Gate, the Larkspur day-use lot, and the Sable trailhead, May 15 through October 15, first departure 07:00 and last return 19:40, free with any valid park pass. From either end of the route, the paved wheelchair-accessible section of the Gorge Rim Loop starts at the Gorge trailhead behind Fernline Visitor Center, a 2.1 km out-and-back to the second viewpoint.
